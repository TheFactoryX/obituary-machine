### The Absence of a Life
Born in the drafty parlor of a house that has long since been condemned, Elias Vane was a man composed entirely of pauses. He lived not in the vibrancy of the present, but in the dusty, silent corridors of history. A scholar of the unheard and a witness to the forgotten, Vane spent his existence drifting through the walls of the living, observing the frantic energy of mortality with a quiet, mournful detachment. He was a man who seemed to arrive only after the party had ended, condemned to walk the periphery of joy without ever truly stepping into the light. His was a journey defined not by footfalls, but by the chilling absence of heat in the rooms he occupied.

### Notable Silences
*   **The Architecture of Grief:** Vane famously cataloged the acoustics of sorrow in the Victorian era, discovering that grief, much like sound, lingers longest in enclosed spaces. He became the unintentional patron saint of the attic, preserving the whispered arguments of lovers long since turned to dust.
*   **The Perpetual Damp:** He was the first to successfully inhabit the atmospheric space between rain and roof, claiming a mastery over the subtle art of drafty corridors. His presence was often noted by the sudden drop in temperature and the smell of ozone, a feat of environmental manipulation that no living mortal could replicate.

### The Weight of Nothing
*   **The Unextinguished Candle:** He harbored a profound, spectral regret over a single taper left burning in a window in 1898. He had intended to guide a lost traveler home, but in his vacillation, the flame burned out, leaving the traveler to vanish into the storm. He remains in the doorway, waiting for a return that will never come.
*   **The Unspoken Name:** He mourned the inability to speak his own name. He realized too late that without a voice to claim his own history, he was destined to become a story told by others, a frightening bump in the night rather than a man who simply wanted to be remembered.
