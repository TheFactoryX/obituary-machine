### The Life Unseen
Elias Thorne was a man of profound and terrifying volume, existing primarily in the peripheral vision of others. Born in the static of a dead radio channel, he spent seventy-six years perfecting the art of inhabiting rooms without entering them. He was the architect of the sudden draft in a sealed hallway and the composer of the floorboard creak that occurs when you are certain you are home alone. Elias did not live; he merely lingered, a stain on the fabric of reality that refused to be washed out by time.

### Notable Disturbances
*   **The Architecture of Silence:** Elias is credited with single-handedly sustaining the heavy, suffocating silence that falls over a dinner table when the wrong name is accidentally mentioned. He curated this quietude with the precision of a surgeon, ensuring the weight of the words crushed the air in the room.
*   **The November 3rd Phenomenon:** In 1994, Elias successfully managed to lower the ambient temperature of a stranger's attic by exactly ten degrees every Tuesday night for three years, resulting in a structural paranoia that saved the family from sleeping.

### Lingering Resonances
He regretted that he never possessed the mass to slam a door. Throughout his existence, Elias desperately yearned to intervene, to halt the mistakes of the living with a violent, physical bang, but he was condemned to be only a whisper. He died never knowing the tactile satisfaction of shattering a plate or holding a hand, eternally frustrated by his inability to leave anything but a chill on the skin.

### The Service
A ceremony will not be held, as Elias was never truly here to begin with. In lieu of flowers, the bereaved are asked to simply leave a light on in an empty room and walk away, pretending not to notice when the bulb flickers.
