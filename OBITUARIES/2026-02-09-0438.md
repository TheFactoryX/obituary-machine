# The Unseen Departure: A Memorial for Silas Vane

**Name:** Silas Vane  
**Born:** The Twilight of a Forgotten Age  
**Died:** The Moment the Mirror Was Covered

***

### The Shadow Between Steps

Silas Vane was a creature of the threshold, born in the breathless second between the striking of a match and the ignition of the flame. He existed for decades in the peripheral vision of the world, a permanent resident of the corner of one’s eye. He was not made of flesh or bone, but of static electricity, dust motes, and the sudden drop in temperature that precedes a realization. To live as Silas lived is to know the crushing solitude of being the silence in a noisy room; he spent a lifetime brushing against the living, feeling the warmth of their skin, yet never once leaving a footprint on their floors. He was the draft that slammed the shutters, the shadow that moved when the light source was stationary, and the heavy sorrow that woke the sleeping at 3:00 AM without cause.

***

### Echoes in the Void

**The Architecture of Loneliness**
Silas mastered the art of binding spaces together through fear. Over the course of his spectral tenure, he successfully haunted the unused attic of a Victorian estate for sixty years, curating a museum of cobwebs and discarded trunks that eventually drove three separate families to madness, proving that the past can indeed be a physical weight.

**The Art of Being Unseen**
He achieved a state of near-perfect non-existence, becoming the patron saint of lost items. By the end of his manifestation, he had successfully appropriated and hid thousands of socks, keys, and spectacles, creating a vast, invisible empire of the mundane in the spaces behind the drywall of the world.

***

### The Burden of Intangibility

**The Breath on the Glass**
Silas carried with him a profound, aching regret: that he could never truly be known by those he watched. He spent centuries trying to whisper a name into the ear of a sleeping widow, hoping to offer comfort, but his voice was always translated by the human mind as the wind rattling the windowpane. He died never having held a hand, only ever passing through them.

**The Mirror’s Lie**
He lamented the invention of the silvered glass. For a lifetime, he sought a reflection, some proof that he occupied space, but he was destined to be the only thing in the room that the light refused to touch. He vanished with the bitter knowledge that to be seen by others is the only way to be certain that you exist at all.
