# The Passing of the Void

**Name:** Elias Thorne  
**Born:** October 14, 1842  
**Died:** November 3, 1912  
**Location:** The Ruins of Blackwood Hall

***

### The Life Unlived
Elias Thorne was a man composed of silences and shallow breaths, a figure who moved through the world with the terrifying lightness of a shadow. Born to a family of weavers, he spent seventy years perfecting the art of being less substantial than air. He did not live; he merely occurred. He was a spectator to his own existence, haunting the hallways of his ancestral home long before his heart ceased its rhythmic, unenthusiastic ticking. He was a ghost in the machine of life, practicing for the afterlife while barely tethered to the living one.

### Notable Achievements
*   **The Architecture of Absence:** Elias successfully renovated the West Wing of Blackwood Hall into a labyrinth of dust sheets and closed drapes, ensuring that no sunlight touched the floorboards for nearly four decades.
*   **The Erasure of Self:** Over the course of his life, he systematically burned every diary, letter, and photograph he ever possessed, achieving a level of historical anonymity that most hermits can only aspire to. He left no footprint, only a draft.
